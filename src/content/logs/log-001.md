  <h1 class="writing-title">
    The Imagination Engine
  </h1>

  <p class="writing-meta">
    May 5, 2026 · writing
  </p>

  <div class="writing-content">

    <p>
      Imagine a great sky falling. Or imagine your yourself become a cat no school or work just meow meow. Now imagine the fundamental substance of the universe – a ubiquitous glob that makes up everything. Or imagine a magical and incredible force that connects every cause with its effect.
    </p>

    <p>
      According to David Hume, our imagination is the single most important faculty we possess. It allows us to think and behave as humans, at all.
    </p>

    <p>
      Hume is perhaps the most radical of the empiricists. He is often thought to argue that anything which is not directly experienced by our senses therefore needs an act of creativity. Everything we cannot immediately see, hear or touch must be the product of our imagination.
    </p>

    <p>
      If we live by such radical empiricism, we quickly see how much of our everyday life is made up by these imaginative fictions. When you press a switch, and the light comes on, you don't 'see' the causality at play, you imagine it. If you see a dragon, you see greatness and his wings - the composite object of “dragon” construct western myth. When we reflect on who we are as individuals, we don't find an essence to our personality, we find momentary memories, sensations, emotions, and so on. When you imagine a “guitar” you are 'creating' an abstract concept of a guitar, not one as it actually appears.
    </p>

    <p>
      In all of the most foundational aspects of our everyday being, we deal in fictions which go far beyond the evidence presented.
    </p>

    <p>
      But this isn't simply on an individual level. Collectively, we each create and sign up to fictions. Laws, property rights, the value of life, the need for governments at all, and so on – these are not experientially known, they are a product of our collective imaginations.
    </p>

    <p>
      Imagination is a wondrous gift. It gives us Ghost, Mythology and more. It allows us to meet your favorite waifu and Gundam or Power Rangers. But, it’s also the bedrock of our entire epistemology. It paints in the vast, unknown gaps between our experiences. Without imagination, we aren't completely human.
    </p>

  </div>
